In the recent years, CO2 emission has become a big topic in the world, a significant challenge for policymakers and environmental advocates.Like many regions worldwide, the European nations have worked hard to decrease CO2 emissions quickly to reduce the effects of climate change.

Understanding the History

The journey begins by looking at the historical data, represented by a line plot showcasing the total CO2 emission in Europe each year from 1960 to 2018. By looking at the plot we observe a huge increase in CO2 emission in 1990, where the lowest CO2 emission in EU after 1990 was in 2015.

Understanding the Peaks

Why did CO2 emissions peak in 1990, and why were they at their lowest in 2015 afterwards?. In 1990, there happened an industrial expasion, where energy consumption icreased, and a growing reliance on fossil fuels during this period.

Following the year of 1990's peak, a significant deacrease in CO2 emission occured, marking a notable achievement in Europe CO2 emissions. When we dive into 1990 and look at the map, we see that Luxembourg had the highest CO2 emission in 1990.

Steps Towards Reduction

Despite the alarming rise in emissions in 1990, the years afterwards shown a gradual decline. This reduction can be attributed to advancements in renewable energy technologies, stricter environmental regulations, and awareness of climate change impacts.
